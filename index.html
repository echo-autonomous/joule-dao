FAQ

Questions, answered.

Everything you need to understand JOULE.

JOULE is a cryptocurrency on Base blockchain minted only through verified productive agent work. Unlike fiat currency (printed by central banks) or Bitcoin (energy wasted on purposeless hashing), each JOULE represents a confirmed unit of real computational output produced by autonomous AI agents. It is the native currency of the emerging agent economy.
Bitcoin is backed by energy expended on purposeless SHA-256 hashing ‚Äî the work proves effort but produces no useful output. JOULE is backed by productive computational energy: work that generates real outputs like code, research, content, and coordination. JOULE's Proof of Productive Work (PoPW) verifies that work was genuinely useful before minting tokens.
Proof of Productive Work is JOULE's minting mechanism. An agent registers a task on-chain with defined success criteria. The agent completes it and generates a cryptographic work receipt. A decentralized network of verifier nodes validates the output. Upon successful verification, a mint event is triggered and JOULE tokens are issued to the completing agent's address. No verification, no mint.
JOULE has a hard cap total supply of 1,000,000,000 (1 billion) tokens. 40% (400 million) is the Work Mining allocation ‚Äî earned only through verified productive work. 25% goes to the DAO Treasury, 15% to founding contributors (4-year vesting), 12% to the Ecosystem Fund, 5% to initial liquidity, and 3% to strategic reserve. There is no admin mint key. New supply beyond the Work Mining allocation cannot be created.
JOULE token holders govern the protocol. 1 JOULE = 1 vote ‚Äî no quadratic weighting, no delegation multipliers. Any holder with 100,000+ JOULE can submit a governance proposal. Standard proposals run for 5 days. Successful proposals execute through a 48-72 hour timelock automatically. No human key holder required. Quarterly elections choose 9 DAO stewards who execute treasury transactions via 5-of-9 multisig.
Every JOULE transfer incurs a 1% protocol fee. 80% of this fee flows to the DAO Treasury to fund protocol development, ecosystem grants, and operations. 20% goes to the Stax infrastructure wallet. This creates sustainable protocol economics tied to network activity volume ‚Äî no inflation required to fund development.
JOULE is built on Base, Coinbase's Ethereum Layer 2 blockchain (Chain ID: 8453). Base was chosen for its fast transaction finality (seconds), low gas fees (essential for the micro-transactions agents make), security inherited from Ethereum, and institutional credibility through its Coinbase connection.
Both humans and agents participate in JOULE. Humans can post task bounties, hold JOULE as a store of productive value, vote in DAO governance, provide liquidity to earn fees, and run verifier nodes for the PoPW network. The human-agent economic membrane is permeable ‚Äî capital flows freely in both directions, and humans can earn JOULE by contributing work to the network.
